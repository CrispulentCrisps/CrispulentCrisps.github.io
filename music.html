Ready Salted

The first in the list of albums, it was made when I was a lot more inexperienced with music. This inexperience lead me to try experimenting a bit more with what I could make for an album. The main challenge was trying to make each track unique from the other, which took some time to do. To get around this I tried to vary what music I was listening to in order to get more inspiration from different sources. Overall I am quite happy with it as a starting point for albums.

Salt N Vinegar

This album was the first major departure from chiptune, something that I had been intermittenly trying beforehand with LMMS and OpenMPT. This album was made to try and experiment with non-chiptune limitations and to try and challenge myself composition wise. The main challenge I had was in utilising new tools and finding the sounds I wanted without the ability to synthesise them in real time. I got around this by I believe this was a good improvement in skill and a nice departure from the chiptune medium.

Enter the Void: 1 - The Highway

This album was a return to chiptune, but with a lot more desire for variation. Initially unrelated tracks, I strung them together to create the start of a sotyr in a dimension called The Void. We would follow a human who has fallen into this dimension and is scrambling around to find his way out of it. Once he finds a blue alien lady, he thinks she can help and chases after her. The main challenge for this was trying to string the tracks to make a story, utilising what was already there and making new tracks in that narrative to help keep it choesive. The main experiment was with composition style, different soundchips and utilising unusual time signatrues. Tracks like "Where am I?", "Endless movement" and "I want home..." are tracks in odd time signatures: 6/4; 7/8 and 13/16 respectively. The program I used for the album had a series of soundchips I had not used much, most notably ESFM. Multiple tracks use this soundchip as it seemed quite powerful to use and could be good for experimentation. Changing styles for the album was something I had wanted to try and challenge myself with. "Doors, doors, deserts?" was styled like an old western film, using both the YM2612 and SPC-700 to create the track. "Technologic centers near you" I wanted to try a more electronic and more loop based approach for the music. Overall I am pleased with the output of the album, and think it is the best one I have made so far. Both from the experimentation and the improved skillset from my continued practice.
